To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing pursuant to my rights under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. §1681), specifically §605(a)(4), which requires that obsolete adverse information not be reported on a consumer credit file beyond seven (7) years from the date of the original delinquency.

A collection account from Midland Credit Management in the amount of $1,847.00 is currently appearing on my Equifax credit report with a reported date of first delinquency of July 2021. However, I have documentation showing that the original delinquency on this account occurred in March 2016, which would place this account beyond the 7-year reporting period.

This appears to be a clear case of re-aging, which is prohibited under FCRA §605. I am requesting that this account be deleted from my credit file immediately.

Please investigate this matter within the 30-day period required by FCRA §611(a)(1)(A) and provide written confirmation of the outcome to the address above.

Why Uline first?
L

Three reasons:

Approval odds: Uline approves ~95% of new businesses with a D-U-N-S. No personal guarantee, no hard pull.
Reports fast: They report to D&B within 30 days of your first invoice.
Low threshold: Just a $50 order qualifies. Order packing supplies, shipping labels — stuff you'd buy anyway.
